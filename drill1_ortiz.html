Beginner's Guide
to Chess

Four squares to start on: who plays, what the pieces do, what's on the board, and how to open well.

Players

Chess is played between two people, one commanding the light pieces, one the dark. They take turns until one king falls.

  • White moves first, then the players alternate turns.
  • The objective is to checkmate the opponent's king.
  • Games can also end in a draw — stalemate, repetition, or agreement.

Rules

Every piece follows its own pattern of movement, and a handful of special moves keep the game sharp.

  • Pawns move forward, bishops diagonally, knights in an L.
  • "Check" means the king is under attack and must be defended.
  • Special moves: castling, en passant, and pawn promotion.

Equipment

All you need is a board, a set of pieces, and — if you're keeping score — a pen.

  • An 8×8 board of 64 alternating light and dark squares.
  • 16 pieces per side: king, queen, 2 rooks, 2 bishops, 2 knights, 8 pawns.
  • Optional: a chess clock and a scoresheet for notation.

Tips

A few habits separate a shaky opening from a confident one.

  • Control the center early — d4, d5, e4, e5 matter most.
  • Develop knights and bishops before bringing out your queen.
  • Castle early to keep your king safe.
